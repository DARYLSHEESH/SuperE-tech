Lesson 1: Information and Communication Technology

In this lesson, we explored the global emergence of ICT and its evolution from Web 1.0 to Web 3.0, emphasizing key trends such as convergence, social media, mobile technologies, and assistive media. The key takeaway is that ICT is continually advancing, becoming increasingly user-focused, interactive, and inclusive.

Lesson 2: Online Safety and Security

In this lesson, our teacher discussed online safety and security in the context of ICT, covering topics such as types of information and associated risks, tips for staying safe online, internet threats, protecting our online reputation, and copyright infringement, along with various cyber risks we may encounter. We learned that sharing excessive personal information could lead to identity theft, entering bank details on phishing sites could jeopardize our savings, and downloading content from untrustworthy websites could slow down our computer.

Lesson 3: Search Engine

In this lesson, we explored the use of search engines, the structure of URLs and their components, tips for conducting effective online research, and the meaning of various symbols like ("", @, #, (), +). We learned that search engines are essential tools for locating online information, while URLs serve as web page addresses with key elements such as protocol and domain name. These skills collectively improve our ability to search for and utilize information on the internet.

Lesson 5: Principles and Basic Techniquies of Image Manipulation

This lesson introduces image manipulation, its principles and techniques, and explores various image hosting platforms. Lesson 6: Mail Merge and Label Generation

This lesson covers Mail Merge, a useful tool for generating personalized documents like letters, emails, and labels. Lesson 7: Custom Animation and Timing

This lesson covers custom animation, including how to apply it, the different types of animation effects, and the importance of timing in animations. Lesson 8: Advance Productive Tool Hyperlink (PPT)

This lesson focuses on defining a hyperlink, linking to a website, linking to a specific section within a document, and linking to an email address, as well as changing the color of hyperlinks. We learned that hyperlinks are essential for connecting related content, making it easier for users to navigate between various pieces of information or resources. To link a website, you usually place the URL between the (<)a href="URL"(>) and tags (in HTML), or use the corresponding feature in a word processor or email client. Mastering the creation of hyperlinks enhances navigation, facilitates information sharing, and improves communication and accessibility both online and in documents.

Lesson 9: Intergrating Images and External Materials in Word Processing

In this lesson, we explored the different types of materials needed for integrating images into documents, such as pictures, clip art, shapes, SmartArt, charts, and screenshots. We also looked at the various ways to place images and the different file formats available. By the end of the lesson, we understood how adding images and external materials to our word processing documents can significantly improve readability, visual appeal, and overall impact. With the tips and strategies shared in this lesson, we now know how to effectively incorporate these elements into our documents, creating engaging and informative content that truly makes an impression.

Lesson 10: Embedded Date and Text

In this lesson, we explored the concept of embedded text and data, learning how it can enhance documents and media, as well as its importance in web development and security. We discovered that embedded text and data are essentially hidden pieces of information within files or objects, providing extra context, functionality, and security. For example, it improves documents by adding things like metadata, hyperlinks, and watermarks, and it enhances media by embedding videos, images, and audio into websites. Moreover, embedded data plays a crucial role in security and privacy through techniques like steganography, digital watermarking, and data encryption. Understanding how embedded text and data work, along with their applications and potential risks, is key to navigating the digital world safely and effectively.

In this lesson, we covered what Excel is, including both simple and complex formulas, its key elements, and the five different types of operators it uses. As we continue to explore and learn more about its features, we can unlock its full potential and use it to achieve our goals more efficiently.
